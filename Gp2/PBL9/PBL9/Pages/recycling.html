Together we make the park greener.

Your simple act of recycling helps the parks to grow again into reality. Every bottle or wrapper placed in the right bin makes a real difference. Let’s work hand in hand to keep our shared spaces clean and thriving.

Recycling Tips and Track Suggestions for Parks

Encourage visitors to reduce waste in the parks with fun and easy recycling tips. Small actions like sorting plastic and paper can greatly reduce pollution. Let’s inspire everyone to keep our parks fresh, clean, and enjoyable for all.

Changing Waste Materials Into New Life

Paper: Recyclable materials that can be turned into notebooks, packaging, or cardboard boxes.

Metal: Can be melted and reused for construction, appliances, or new cans.

Plastics: Recyclable plastics can be turned into realistic plant pot, container and plastics lamp.

Glass: Can be melted to create new bottles. You can even make light holders or plant pot as recycling

Understanding More about 3R

Reduce

Minimize waste generation and resource consumption at the source.

  • Avoid the by one get one offer,discount
  • Reduce the use of product
  • Use less to reduce waste

Avoid “items you won’t actually use.”- Examples: Bring your own water bottle instead of buying drinks.

Reuse

Extend the life of products by finding new uses for them.

  • Using the product again after it had used
  • Use things again before throwing them away
  • Examples: Reusable snack containers, refillable bottles.

Ask yourself before throwing an item, sometimes the items that you think are useless may be pretty useful in life. Extend the life of your products and avoid creating new waste.

Recycle

Process materials to create new products instead of discarding them.

  • start collecting cardboard, mixed paper products, plastics, turning those into raw components to make new product.
  • You can restore back your recycled product back into the park to make the park alive again
  • Example: Old paper can be pulped and processed into new paper again. Old bottles can be used to make plant pots and light holders.

Eco-habits for the park vistors

Follow for more tips

Do this Habits before coming to parks

01

Pack reusable items: Bring your own reusable water bottles, coffee mugs, and bags to minimize single-use plastics.

02

Minimize packaging: Choose snacks and supplies with minimumn packaging when pack snacks for your picnic.

03

Check park guidelines: Look up the specific recycling rules for the park you're visiting.

04

Review what not to recycle: Be aware of items that are not recyclable in public bins, such as food-soiled containers, plastic bags, foam, and batteries.

Do this Habits when you are in the parks

01

Look for the bins: Locate the recycling bins whenever you want to throw the trash. Recycling bins can be found at the busy areas like picnic spots, car parking, and concession areas.

02

Follow the signs: Pay close attention to the signs on each bin to ensure you are sorting your waste in the correct bin. Ask for help, if you are not sure which bin to throw

03

Be respectful to nature: Do not throw your trash carelessly.Make sure you double check your waste is already in the bin or not.

04

Save space in the bins: Flatten items like bottles or boxes before throwing them away so the bins don't fill up too quickly and more people can use them.

Do this Habits When you leave the Park

01

Take your trash: If you can't find a recycling bin or if the bins are full, take your waste with you to dispose of it later at home.

02

Leave no trace: Always leave the park as clean or cleaner Do not leave plastic wraps or food scraps behind.

03

Double-Check: before leaving, check around your area, to make sure you haven't dropped any samll items like bottle caps, tissues, or trash.

04

Ask your self: Don't let your small action make the nature beauty disspear. Always take leftover food with you instead of leaving it behind.

Benefits of recycling in parks

Environmental Benefits

  • With recycling Tips, we create a healthier planet for ourselves
  • By recycling, we use fewer raw materials. Also, it helps to protect and conserve natural resources.
  • By recycling, it can also conserves energy. For example, recyling just 10 plastics bottles save enough energy to power a laptop for more than 25 hours.

Community benefits

  • In parks, there are some areas that are cover with scatter trash.
  • Recycling provides these areas with a healthier and more sustainable alternative
  • As a result, the parks will be cleaner. Many people will visit parks more often

Economy Benefits

  • Less litter means fewer clean-up efforts, saving time and money for park services.
  • With recycling it helps to reduce the dispoal waste management cost for community especially in local parks.
  • Recycling in public offeres many job opportunities for locals.
