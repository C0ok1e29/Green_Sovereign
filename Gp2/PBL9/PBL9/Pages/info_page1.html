Park Etiquette: Respecting Nature

A guide to protecting our shared natural spaces.

The Importance of Staying on Trails

In a park setting, it is vital to stay on marked footpaths or trails. Straying from these designated areas tramples delicate undergrowth and contributes to soil compaction and erosion, especially near riverbanks or scenic overlooks. Remember that plants are there for everyone to enjoy; never pick wildflowers or break tree branches.

When encountering park wildlife like deer, racoons, or local birds, do not approach them. Feeding park animals can make them dependent on humans and cause health issues.

Park Ecosystem Summary

To show respect for the park's ecosystem, always remain on official, marked trails to protect the integrity of the soil and plant life. Treat the park's natural elements as a shared resource: leave all plants and natural objects untouched. When enjoying the scenic views, give all park wildlife a wide berth; observe them silently and refrain from feeding them, helping keep the park's animals healthy and naturally wild.

Core Rules for Preservation

  • Stick to the designated park paths.
  • Keep your feet off the flower beds.
  • Do not pick wildflowers or collect leaves.
  • Observe the squirrels and birds quietly.
  • Never feed the ducks or other park animals.
  • Leave the park stones and logs in place.
  • Preserve the park's natural features.
  • Stay behind all park fences and barriers.
