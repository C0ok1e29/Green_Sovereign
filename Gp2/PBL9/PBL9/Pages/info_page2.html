Eco-Friendly Personal Habits

Bringing reusables, saving travel energy, and park stewardship.

Summary: Be a Park Steward

Ensure your personal habits support a sustainable park environment. Minimize park waste by bringing your own reusable items (bottles, containers) and meticulously taking all trash with you when you leave the picnic area or trail. This simple action prevents litter from polluting the natural landscape, protects wildlife from ingesting harmful materials, and reduces the workload on park staff. Remember the principle: Take only pictures, leave only footprints.

Reduce air pollution by saving energy in your travel choices, prioritizing walking, biking, or public transit to access the park. Transportation is a significant source of urban emissions; choosing sustainable methods directly improves the air quality for park visitors and the surrounding community. Finally, actively engage in park stewardship by joining local community clean-ups to help keep the park beautiful and litter-free, strengthening your connection to the environment.

Quick Eco-Tips

  • Use park bins for waste, or pack it out.
  • Refill your water bottle at the park fountain.
  • Bring your lunch in a reusable container for a park picnic.
  • Walk, bike, or use transit to visit the park.
  • Tidy up your picnic area completely.
  • Join a "Friends of the Park" clean-up event.
  • Recycle glass and plastic bottles at the park's stations.
  • Turn off lights if you are using a park facility (like a restroom).

Minimize Your Waste Impact

Minimize your waste impact during your park visit by embracing a "Zero-Waste Picnic" approach. Always bring a reusable water bottle and packed food in durable containers to avoid disposable wrappers and plastic bags. By eliminating single-use plastics, you directly reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills or, worse, polluting the park's natural habitats.

When you are finished, meticulously "pack out" anything you "packed in." This means all food scraps, wrappers, and even small tissues should leave the park with you, or be properly placed in designated recycling and waste bins. Furthermore, save energy by thinking about your journey: walk, cycle, or take a bus to the park entrance instead of driving. These choices reduce traffic congestion, improve the park's air quality, and lessen your overall carbon footprint for the day.
